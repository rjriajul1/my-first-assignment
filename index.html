pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

Bangladesh 2.0 innovation emphasizes technology, sustainability, and entrepreneurship to build a resilient and inclusive future.

Sustainability

Bangladesh 2.0 sustainability focuses on eco-friendly practices, renewable energy, and resource efficiency to ensure long-term environmental and economic resilience.

Infrastructure

Bangladesh 2.0 infrastructure aims to develop modern, sustainable, and inclusive systems to support economic growth and improve quality of life.

Empowerment

The empowerment pillar of Bangladesh 2.0 focuses on fostering inclusivity, gender equality, and access to opportunities for sustainable societal progress.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in
renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Dr. Muhammad Yunus is a Bangladeshi social entrepreneur, economist, and Nobel Peace Prize laureate, known as the "Father of Microfinance." He founded Grameen Bank in 1983, pioneering microcredit to provide small loans to impoverished individuals, especially women, to help them achieve financial independence. His innovative approach has lifted millions out of poverty and inspired similar initiatives worldwide. Dr. Yunus continues to advocate for social business and sustainable development to address global challenges.
